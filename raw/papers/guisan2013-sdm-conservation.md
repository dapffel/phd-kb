# Predicting species distributions for conservation decisions

**Authors:** Antoine Guisan, Reid Tingley, John B. Baumgartner, Ilona Naujokaitis-Lewis, Patricia R. Sutcliffe, Ayesha I.T. Tulloch, Tracey J. Regan, Lluis Brotons, Eve McDonald-Madden, Chrystal Mantyka-Pringle, Tara G. Martin, Jonathan R. Rhodes, Ramona Maggini, Samantha A. Setterfield, Jane Elith, Mark W. Schwartz, Brendan A. Wintle, Olivier Broennimann, Mike Austin, Simon Ferrier, Michael R. Kearney, Hugh P. Possingham, Yvonne M. Buckley
**Journal:** Ecology Letters, 16(12), 1424–1435
**Year:** 2013
**DOI:** 10.1111/ele.12189

## Summary

Despite widespread claims of applicability, evidence of SDMs supporting real-world conservation decisions is scarce in peer-reviewed literature (<1% of SDM papers target conservation decisions). The authors show successful examples exist but are hidden in grey literature. They propose that SDMs should be embedded within structured decision-making frameworks, with case studies from four conservation domains: biological invasions, critical habitat identification, reserve selection, and translocation.

## Key Points

- SDMs can inform most stages of structured decision making: problem identification, objective definition, defining possible actions, evaluating consequences, and assessing trade-offs.
- Modellers need to understand the decision process; decision makers need to provide feedback to modellers. "Translators" between the two groups could facilitate this.
- Both false positives and false negatives have costs that vary by decision context (e.g., for invasive species, false negatives at pre-border stage are more serious).
- Uncertainty quantification is critical but often incomplete — ensemble GCM projections capture only one source of uncertainty, not model construction, species data errors, or SDM goodness-of-fit.
- Tools like Marxan and Zonation can incorporate SDM uncertainty into conservation prioritization.
- Time urgency affects whether investing in SDM construction is worthwhile vs. immediate action.
- Population dynamics models may need to be combined with SDMs when persistence predictions are required.

## Real-world Examples

- Australia: SDMs used in weed risk assessment and listing gamba grass as a weed (Northern Territory)
- Canada: Hybrid SDM-population models for Ord's kangaroo rat critical habitat (39% of predicted suitable habitat unlikely to support population viability)
- Catalonia: SDMs for threatened birds influenced a legal decree in Natura 2000 framework
- Madagascar: SDMs for priority conservation areas protected by ministerial decree
- NSW Australia: SDMs for 2300+ species in northeast forests, integrated into environmental decision-support system for reserve selection
