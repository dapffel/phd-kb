Thanks for sharing the Bates et al. paper — I found the argument about the shift from pattern extraction to design-and-inference compelling, especially the point that AI commodifies the modelling step but can't tell you when to trust the output. It got me thinking about how to operationalise model interrogation systematically for SDMs. I have an idea for a pipeline: (1) use an LLM to extract experiment metadata from published SDM papers (species traits, algorithm, predictors, evaluation approach, spatial extent), (2) automatically generate virtual species simulations that mirror each experiment's design, and (3) compare real versus virtual results to map where they converge or diverge. The practical payoff is a decision tool — where results converge, simulation is a cheap reliable proxy; where they diverge, it flags the specific conditions (taxa, regions, scales) where real field data are irreplaceable. Essentially it could help answer: when do we need to spend money on fieldwork, and when can we safely simulate?
