## Ingested Sources

- **[[araujo2007-ensemble-forecasting]]** — Araújo & New (2007). Review advocating ensemble forecasting of species distributions using bounding box, consensus, and probabilistic approaches.
- **[[allouche2006-tss-accuracy]]** — Allouche, Tsoar & Kadmon (2006). Introduces TSS as prevalence-independent accuracy measure for SDMs, replacing kappa.
- **[[elith2009-sdm-review]]** — Elith & Leathwick (2009). Broad review of SDMs: history, methods (GLMs to machine learning), scale, extrapolation, and future directions.
- **[[efron1993-bootstrap]]** — Efron & Tibshirani (1993). Textbook introducing bootstrap resampling for statistical inference. (Partial excerpt only.)
- **[[elith2010-range-shifting]]** — Elith, Kearney & Phillips (2010). Cane toad case study showing data treatment matters more than algorithm choice; MESS for extrapolation diagnosis; smoother models extrapolate better.
- **[[guisan2013-sdm-conservation]]** — Guisan et al. (2013). <1% of SDM papers inform real conservation decisions. Proposes structured decision-making framework with case studies in invasions, critical habitat, reserves, and translocation.
- **[[meyer2021-area-of-applicability]]** — Meyer & Pebesma (2021). Area of applicability via dissimilarity index in weighted predictor space. CV error holds within AOA; outside it, predictions unreliable.
- **[[roberts2017-cross-validation]]** — Roberts et al. (2017). Random CV underestimates error on structured data. Block CV by space, time, groups, or phylogeny provides realistic error estimates.
- **[[thuiller2005-climate-change-plants]]** — Thuiller et al. (2005). 1,350 European plants under 7 climate scenarios. >50% vulnerable by 2080. Mountains most sensitive. BIOMOD ensemble framework.
- **[[peterson2011-niches-distributions]]** — Peterson et al. (2011). Monograph formalising niche-distribution theory: BAM diagram, Grinnellian vs Eltonian niches, scenopoetic variables, E/G-space duality.
