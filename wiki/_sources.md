## Ingested Sources

- **[[GEB-2026-macroecology]]** — Bates et al. (2026). Perspective arguing macroecology's value has shifted from pattern extraction to design-and-inference; AI commodifies the workflow middle, relocating intellectual labour to study design and model interrogation.

- **[[araujo2006-sdm-challenges]]** — Araújo & Guisan (2006). Essay identifying five challenges for SDMs: niche concept clarification, sampling design, parameterization, model selection/predictor contribution, and model evaluation. Argues fundamental/realized niche dichotomy is artificial; endorses Chase & Leibold's Grinnellian definition.
- **[[araujo2007-ensemble-forecasting]]** — Araújo & New (2007). Review advocating ensemble forecasting of species distributions using bounding box, consensus, and probabilistic approaches.
- **[[allouche2006-tss-accuracy]]** — Allouche, Tsoar & Kadmon (2006). Introduces TSS as prevalence-independent accuracy measure for SDMs, replacing kappa.
- **[[efron1993-bootstrap]]** — Efron & Tibshirani (1993). Textbook introducing bootstrap resampling for statistical inference. (Partial excerpt only.)
- **[[elith2009-sdm-review]]** — Elith & Leathwick (2009). Broad review of SDMs: history, methods (GLMs to machine learning), scale, extrapolation, and future directions.
- **[[elith2010-range-shifting]]** — Elith, Kearney & Phillips (2010). Cane toad case study showing data treatment matters more than algorithm choice; MESS for extrapolation diagnosis; smoother models extrapolate better.
- **[[fitzpatrick2009-non-analog-climate]]** — Fitzpatrick & Hargrove (2009). Proposes "power of prediction analysis" to identify non-analog environments where SDM projections are invalid; two case studies (Caspian Sea→Great Lakes, SW Australia under climate change).
- **[[guisan2013-sdm-conservation]]** — Guisan et al. (2013). <1% of SDM papers inform real conservation decisions. Proposes structured decision-making framework with case studies in invasions, critical habitat, reserves, and translocation.
- **[[meyer2021-area-of-applicability]]** — Meyer & Pebesma (2021). Area of applicability via dissimilarity index in weighted predictor space. CV error holds within AOA; outside it, predictions unreliable.
- **[[pearman2008-niche-dynamics]]** — Pearman, Guisan, Broennimann & Randin (2008). Review of niche conservatism vs. niche shifts across timescales from decades to millions of years. Evidence is mixed; combining SDMs, phylogenetics, and evolutionary models can identify species for which niche stasis is supportable.
- **[[peterson2011-niches-distributions]]** — Peterson et al. (2011). Monograph formalising niche-distribution theory: BAM diagram, Grinnellian vs Eltonian niches, scenopoetic variables, E/G-space duality.
- **[[roberts2017-cross-validation]]** — Roberts et al. (2017). Random CV underestimates error on structured data. Block CV by space, time, groups, or phylogeny provides realistic error estimates.
- **[[santini2021-sdm-reliability]]** — Santini et al. (2021). Virtual species simulation shows split-sample validation over-estimates true SDM accuracy, binarization severely degrades predictions, and assumption violations create a false sense of accuracy; most published SDM climate projections use problematic practices.
- **[[thuiller2005-climate-change-plants]]** — Thuiller et al. (2005). 1,350 European plants under 7 climate scenarios. >50% vulnerable by 2080. Mountains most sensitive. BIOMOD ensemble framework.
- **[[urban2015-extinction-risk]]** — Urban (2015). Meta-analysis of 131 studies: 7.9% of species predicted extinct from climate change overall; risk accelerates with temperature, threatening one in six species at RCP 8.5. Highest risks in South America and Australia/NZ. Dispersal assumptions and extinction debt thresholds are the largest uncertainty sources after climate trajectory.
- **[[williams2007-novel-climates]]** — Williams & Jackson (2007). Late-glacial no-analog communities linked to novel climates with high seasonality; IPCC ensemble projections show novel climates arising by 2100 primarily in tropics/subtropics; challenges for niche-based ecological forecasting.
- **[[yates2018-model-transferability]]** — Yates, Bouchet, Caley et al. (2018). Fifty experts identify 12 challenges (6 fundamental, 6 technical) for model transferability. Highest-priority gap: standardised transferability metrics. Environmental dissimilarity matters more than geographic distance; no silver-bullet algorithm; mechanistic models promising but untested.
