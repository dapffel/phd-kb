## Summaries

- **[[araujo2007-ensemble-forecasting]]** — Ensemble forecasting of species distributions (Araújo & New, 2007)
- **[[allouche2006-tss-accuracy]]** — TSS as prevalence-independent alternative to kappa for SDM accuracy (Allouche et al., 2006)
- **[[elith2009-sdm-review]]** — Comprehensive review of SDMs across realms, methods, and applications (Elith & Leathwick, 2009)
- **[[efron1993-bootstrap]]** — Introduction to bootstrap resampling for statistical inference (Efron & Tibshirani, 1993)
- **[[elith2010-range-shifting]]** — Modelling range-shifting species: cane toad case study, MESS diagnostics, mechanistic-correlative integration (Elith et al., 2010)
- **[[guisan2013-sdm-conservation]]** — SDMs for conservation decisions: structured decision-making framework across four conservation domains (Guisan et al., 2013)
- **[[meyer2021-area-of-applicability]]** — Area of applicability (AOA) for spatial prediction models via dissimilarity index (Meyer & Pebesma, 2021)
- **[[roberts2017-cross-validation]]** — Block cross-validation for structured ecological data: spatial, temporal, hierarchical, phylogenetic (Roberts et al., 2017)
- **[[thuiller2005-climate-change-plants]]** — Climate change threats to 1,350 European plant species; >50% vulnerable by 2080 (Thuiller et al., 2005)
- **[[peterson2011-niches-distributions]]** — Ecological niches and geographic distributions: BAM diagram, Grinnellian vs Eltonian niches, E/G-space duality (Peterson et al., 2011)

## Concepts

- **[[species-distribution-models]]** — Correlative models relating species occurrence to environmental characteristics; the central methodological framework across 8/10 sources
- **[[model-extrapolation]]** — Predicting beyond training data environments; diagnostics (MESS, DI/AOA), smoother models extrapolate better
- **[[model-evaluation]]** — Accuracy metrics (AUC, TSS, kappa) and cross-validation strategies; block CV recommended for structured data
- **[[ensemble-forecasting]]** — Combining multiple model outputs for robustness; bounding box, consensus, and probabilistic approaches
- **[[ecological-niche]]** — Grinnellian (scenopoetic, geographic) vs Eltonian (linked, local) niches; BAM diagram; fundamental vs realised
